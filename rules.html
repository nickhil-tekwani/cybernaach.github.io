Official Rules

Rules and Regulations

  1. Dancers must be current undergraduate students or recent graduates. Alumni may help create choreo (see rule 4 on biting), but the performances must be done by the competing dancers.
    • Since CyberNaach was founded on the principle of adapting the circuit to a COVID world, we empathize with the Class of 2020 who may not have had an opportunity to participate in their senior year dance season. As a result, Alumni from the Class of 2020 may perform with their undergraduate team, up to a maximum of 20% of the competing perfomers of the team.
  2. No dancer may be repeated in the bracket for two (2) rounds. This means that a dancer who participates in round 1 may not participate again until round 4. (This also means that your team requires a minimum of 7 dancers)
  3. Teams have the option to "shuffle" the mix of one (1) assigned segment throughout the whole competition. A team who wishes to use their "shuffle" must notify the board within 24 hours of the mix being assigned. Requests to shuffle the segment may be considered after 24 hours, up to the discretion of the board and the opposing team.
  4. Choreography should be created in-house as much as possible. Performances should not be reused (without permission from original creators) and should not be old choreo (even if it's your own teams old choreo). If choreo is reused or borrowed from other performances (including if the original choreographer gives permission), it is up to the judges' discretion for removing points from the "Originality/Creativity" section of the rubric.
  5. Videos must be submitted by the deadlines. Any delays in submission may result in disqualification, up to the discretion of the competition board. This rule exists to provide smooth, timely proceeding of the competition as well as no unfair advantage to any team since everyone is given the same amount of time to submit videos.
  6. All dancers must be above the age of 18; however, exceptions may be granted on a case-by-case basis by the board. Reach out to the board with any questions.
  7. No props, unless explicitly stated by the board.
  8. Videos must be high quality (at least 720p), one angle, in landscape orientation, and steady (no movement of camera).
  9. Dancers may not have costumes, team or university gear, or any other university affiliation clearly visible in the frame of the video.
  10. No video editing of any kind.
  11. If the choreo exceeds the length provided, or is outside the segment that was assigned, it will be cut off and judges will not be able to view that part of the video. If choreo is shorter than the segment assigned, points will be deducted from the performance score. Judges will only judge the dance that is submitted within the segments assigned.
  12. If not enough teams sign up for the competition by the deadline (and any extensions), the competition will not take place, and teams will be refunded their registration fees.

Any violations of these rules may lead to automatic disqualification from the competition. Exceptions may be granted at the discretion of the board, in extenuating circumstances, with proper documentation.

* Note: Rules and Rubric are subject to change prior to the competition start date.
