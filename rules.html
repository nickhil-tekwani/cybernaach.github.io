Official Rules

Rules and Regulations

  1. Dancers must be current undergraduate students, no alumni of the team or university. Alumni may help create choreo, but the performances must be done by current students.
  2. Videos must be submitted by the deadlines. Any delays in submission may result in disqualification, up to the discretion of the competition board. This rule exists to provide smooth, timely proceeding of the competition as well as no unfair advantage to any team since everyone is given the same amount of time to submit videos.
  3. Teams may not request non-team members or other teams for individuals to perform for their team. All performers on a team must be from the same university. (However, since dancers must be current university students, individual students who may not have a university team may form their own team, made up of students from their university)
  4. All dancers must be above the age of 18; however, exceptions may be granted on a case-by-case basis by the board. Reach out to the board with any questions.
  5. No props, unless explicitly stated by the board.
  6. Videos must be high quality (at least 720p), one angle, in landscape orientation, and steady (no movement of camera).
  7. Dancers may not have costumes, team or university gear, or any other university affiliation clearly visible in the frame of the video.
  8. No video editing of any kind.
  9. If the choreo exceeds the length provided, or is outside the segment that was assigned, it will be cut off and judges will not be able to view that part of the video. If choreo is shorter than the segment assigned, points will be deducted from the performance score. Judges will only judge the dance that is submitted within the segments assigned.
  10. If not enough teams sign up for the competition by the deadline (and any extensions), the competition will not take place, and teams will be refunded their registration fees.

Any violations of these rules may lead to automatic disqualification from the competition. Exceptions may be granted at the discretion of the board, in extenuating circumstances, with proper documentation.
